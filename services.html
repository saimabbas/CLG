The FDCPA (Fair Debt Collection Practices Act) was put in place to protect consumer rights. The law protects consumers from harassment and unfair collection practices.




What is considered harassment or unfair collection practices?

  • Harassing or threatening phone calls from debt collectors
  • The use of any profanity from the third-party debt collector attempting to collect a debt.
  • Phone calls outside of the hours of 8 AM and 9 PM
  • 7 calls within 7 consecutive days
  • Threatening legal action with no intent
  • Failing to disclose that they are a debt collector
  • Any third party pretending to be a government official or law enforcement
  • Reaching out to friends and family for information
  • Making any knowledge of the debt publicly available.

At Consumer Legal Group, we take these violations seriously, and begin to build a case against the third party debt collectors, to sue them for their violations. In each lawsuit for a violation, the client can be awarded up to $1,000.

What is Validation?

Debt validation is a legal debt resolution process in which creditors need to prove that you owe the funds that appear in your name. It is a basic right to challenge debt that may have been wrongfully attributed to you.
The Fair Debt Collection Practices Act (FDCPA) gives every American the right to contest inaccurate debt by writing an appeal to the collector within 30 days of a collection request.
The collection request can be by phone or writing, but needs to clearly identify the creditor and amount owed. If the first contact is by phone, the collector must notify you in writing within 5 days. In any case, you should act quickly so you don’t miss your window of opportunity.
